About

Who I am

Summary

I am a Computational Biologist and Machine Learning Scientist specializing in AI-driven biological discovery. My work sits at the intersection of large language models, multimodal deep learning, and large-scale biological data — with the goal of mapping how proteins organize inside cells and how mutations drive cancer. I am passionate about building tools that make AI a genuine partner in scientific discovery.

Current Role

Postdoctoral Scientist at Johnson & Johnson (Dec 2025–Present), developing network-based models from multi-omics data to understand immune diseases.

Research Interests

LLM applications in biology · Multimodal ML · Protein network biology · Cancer cell mapping · Agentic AI systems · Perturbation biology

Research & Work

Postdoctoral Scientist
Dec 2025 – Present
Johnson & Johnson · San Diego, CA
  • Developing network-based computational models integrating multi-omics data to identify disease mechanisms in immune disorders.
  • Applying machine learning and systems biology approaches to translate large-scale biological data into therapeutic insights.
Ph.D. Researcher
Jul 2022 – Dec 2025
UC San Diego — Dr. Trey Ideker's Lab · San Diego, CA
  • Developed multimodal deep learning frameworks integrating protein networks and high-resolution imaging data across 5,100+ proteins using self-supervised contrastive learning to create comprehensive human cancer cell maps (Nature, 2025).
  • Integrated cancer genomics data with the cancer cell map to identify 21 recurrently mutated protein systems in pediatric tumors, with 102 driver genes validated by mouse transposon screening.
  • Benchmarked LLMs for biological discovery, designing systematic evaluation frameworks for gene set function analysis (Nature Methods, 2024).
  • Developing agentic AI systems for automated cancer genetic dependency identification and biomedical hypothesis generation using role-specialized LLM agents.
